Once upon a time, the second little pig decided he would build his house out of sticks. He did not want to work as long as his older brother, who was busy stacking heavy bricks, but he also did not want a flimsy house of straw. So he walked into the woods and gathered strong branches and straight sticks. He carried them back one bundle at a time, whistling as he worked. Carefully, he stacked the sticks into walls and laid more across the top for a roof. He wedged small twigs into the gaps and pressed the door tightly into place. When he was done, he stepped back and smiled. “My house is sturdy and fine,” he said proudly. “It will stand up just well enough.” But before long, there came a knock at the door. “Little pig, little pig, let me come in,” said a deep, hungry voice. “Not by the hair of my chinny chin chin!” replied the pig. “Then I’ll huff, and I’ll puff, and I’ll blow your house in!” The wolf took a great big breath. He huffed. He puffed. The stick walls began to tremble and shake. The little pig held his breath as the roof creaked above him. With one enormous puff, the sticks burst apart and flew in every direction. The house tumbled down in a clatter of wood. And the second little pig ran as fast as his little legs could carry him.
